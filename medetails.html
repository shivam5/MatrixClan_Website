General Rules

  • All schools must arrive for registration by 8:30 AM.
  • All students must be in school uniform to avoid disqualification.
  • The school may be represented by only one team in each of the events.
  • Teams may be penalised or disqualified for indiscipline or for not adhering to the rules.
  • Students are not allowed to participate in any clashing events.
  • The events are open to students of classes 9-12.
  • Winning schools must be present at the prize distribution ceremony.
  • Schools may confirm their participation by registering at our website or via the registration forms which may be faxed to Mount St. Mary’s School’s fax number - 011-25695353 by 14th October.

Event Details

Symposium
  • The school will be represented in the symposium by a team consisting of a speaker, an interjector and visual aid supporter.
  • A maximum of 2.5 minutes will be given for speaking.
  • There will be one interjection per speaker and no cross-questioning will be allowed.
  • In the absence of any interjection, the speaker stands to gain full points in the rebuttal criterion.
  • All presentations must be made using Microsoft PowerPoint.
  • Points will also be rewarded for the best interjector and best visual aid supporter.
  • The speaker will be judged on content, clarity, presentation, articulation and rebuttal.
  • The teams must choose a topic from the following:
    • Patents – Intellectual Property Being Monopolised?
    • RBI’s Monetary Policy Affecting The Country’s Growth
    • The Deep Web Leading to Deeper Secrets
    • Public Services @ Private Companies – Dynamic Combination
    • Power of Government over Internet Governance
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 3

Quiz
The quiz event will feature a written preliminary round consisting of questions from the fields of computer science, technology and economics through which the five best teams will be selected for the on-stage final round.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 3

Matrix Mindcraft (Surprise Event)
The Matrix Mindcraft is a surprise event for which the participants must be prepared to battle any quest that might await them in the realm of technology.
  • The event will consist of a preliminary round through which six teams will be selected for the finals.
  • Keep in mind that the only thing that should surprise us is that there are still some things than can surprise us.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 2

Synchronous (Creative Event)
When we are in sync with our team, only then can we create magic. Synchronous, the creative event aims to test the highest levels of teamwork, digital mastery and presentation.
  • Teams will be required to design images and a website on the given topic using the photo library and information given.
  • The task will be disclosed on the spot.
  • Software Provided: Adobe Photoshop CS4, Notepad++, Sublime Text 2 and the latest versions of Mozilla Firefox & Google Chrome.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 2

Programming
The programming event will have a written preliminary round featuring arithmetic, logical and string based problems.
  • Software Provided: Turbo C++ 3.0.
  • The eight top-scoring teams will be selected for the brain-racking on-computer final round.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 2

Gaming
Victory lies in store for those who dare to play.
  • Participants for the gaming event should be prepared for all genres of video games on different platforms.
  • Participants are allowed to bring their own plug-n-play USB controllers, mice and headphones.
  • See you serverside.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 4

Crossword
The cabalistic grid of black and white - the crossword will consist of clues which point to answers in the field of technology.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 2

Brand Advertising
  • The team for Brand Advertising will be given a product with its brand.
  • Their task will be to prepare and enact an advertisement in English.
  • The teams may use any costumes, props, charts, musical instruments, jingles or taglines to present their ad in four minutes on- and off- stage.
  • Registered schools will receive their topics two days prior to the event.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 5

Wrap-o-Nomics
  • Participants will be required to design an informative package of a product containing the name, logo and all the necessary details.
  • The topics will be given on the spot.
  • The school will be providing only cartridge sheets.
  • Number of teams per school: 1
  • Number of participants per team: 2
